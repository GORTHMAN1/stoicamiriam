About me


My name is Stoica Miriam-Luana, I am 19 years old, and I am currently studying acting.

Since I was very young, I have been attracted to art itself, coloring everything I found, decorating things that maybe shouldn't have been decorated, singing anytime and at a quite loud volume, and impersonating famous characters or whoever appeared on TV. All these actions came instinctively, without premeditation or prior thought.



In 7th grade, I discovered an amateur theater course, where I felt very good, where I felt accepted, I felt like I belonged to that team. The people, the atmosphere, this sense of belonging made me love theater. And once contacted, it's very hard to detach yourself from it.

Studies


The journey begins in high school, being a graduate of a vocational school, more precisely the "Ion Vidu" National Art High School in Timișoara.

Currently, I am a student in the Performing Arts department of the Faculty of Music and Theater within the West University of Timișoara, with teaching in German.

In my professional development, workshops I have participated in play an important role. Some of these are:

  • Classic and contemporary dance workshop with Victoriya Medvedeva
  • Biomechanics workshop with Philip Brehze
  • Theater dance workshop with Ötvös Kinga
  • Scenography workshop with Adrian Damian
  • Commedia dell'arte and actor's art workshop with Nicky Wolcz
  • Actor's art workshop with Misi Fazakas
  • Dance and biomechanics workshop with Fatma Mohamed
  • Dance workshop with Beatrice Tudor

Projects


Some of the projects from high school and college:

  • The Rules of the Game, directed by Mirela Puia
  • A Man and Several Women, directed by Cătălin Ursu
  • An Odd Couple, directed by Cătălin Ursu

One of the most significant shows would be "The Lord of the Flies", created by Cătălin Ursu, Raul Bastean, and Mirela Puia for the National Actor's Art Olympiad, where Timișoara obtained first place in the performance section in 2022.


I also had independent shows outside of high school, such as "Gorgo Therapy" at Basca Theater.

Currently, I am acting in the play "Games in the Backyard", directed by Daniel Săvescu, and at college, we are working with Nicky Wolcz on a collage of excerpts from different plays, and for the end of this semester, we are working on "Trust", a production created by Andrea Wolfer and Florin Vidamsky based on Falk Richter's text.

Thoughts on art


For me, theater and art, in general, mean a lot. I couldn't describe what I feel about art, try to define it or explain what it awakens in me, it's ineffable.

I believe that this thing called "art" should not necessarily be beautiful, aesthetic, pleasant, or easy to look at, quite the opposite. I'm not saying that art cannot be like that, of course it can, sometimes this form is more appreciated, but much more to my taste is the disgusting side of art, the grotesque, the slimy part of it. Not being able to look, being disturbed, awakening in me a desire to fight for the things presented to change.

I think the pandemic has been an efficient mentor for me, I once again realized the real importance of art and how society relates to it in such a context. It was sad, disappointing, and dry. Fortunately, there were virtual ways to spread art even in such moments. I believe that's when we needed it the most. Not just me, but people in general.

Over time, I started to find it in everything and to try to create it from anything, and it, in turn, heals me, dresses wounds I didn't even know I had, helps me treat old afflictions that I thought were irreparable. It teaches me to be a better version of myself, and that's why I believe art must be ugly for us to be more beautiful.

I believe that at the core of art, just like at the core of the world, is love. And a very beautiful quote, found in the play "The Family M. Disease" directed by Radu Afrim, which I love and recommend on every occasion, says: "To live is beautiful, but to love is indispensable." So live, love, and consume as much art as possible. It's the only excess I know that has harmed no one.
